Manuel Peter Neuer (born 27 March 1986) is a German professional footballer who plays as a Goalkeeper and captains both Bundesliga club FC Bayern Munich and the Germany National Football Team.

He is regarded as one of the Greatest Goalkeepers in the History of Football.

Neuer has been described as a "sweeper-keeper" because of his playing style of rushing off his line in speed to anticipate opponents, going out of the goalkeeper box.

Neuer made his Germany under-21 debut on 15 August 2006 against the Netherlands. He won the 2009 UEFA European Under-21 Championship with Germany in Sweden and kept a clean sheet in the 4–0 win in the final against England. Neuer was called up on 19 May 2009 to the senior German squad for a tour of Asia. He made his debut on this tour in a match against the United Arab Emirates on 2 June. Neuer was selected as Germany's number one goalkeeper for the 2010 World Cup in South Africa.

Neuer played every minute of every match in the qualification campaign for UEFA Euro 2012. After Germany's 3–1 away win against Turkey, he was especially praised for his "sensational" performance.

Neuer's "sweeper-keeper" playing style distinguished him from other starting goalkeepers in the 2014 World Cup. Germany won the World Cup by defeating Argentina 1–0 in the final. Neuer won the Golden Glove award for the tournament's best goalkeeper.

During UEFA Euro 2016, Neuer did not concede any goals for Germany's Group stage matches. Germany were knocked out in the semi-finals.

Prior to the start of qualification for the 2018 FIFA World Cup, Neuer was named new Captain of the National Team. But Germany were knocked out of the Group stages. Neuer was called up for UEFA Euro 2020 as well.

In June 2021, he achieved his 100th cap with the national team in a friendly match against Latvia, becoming the first German goalkeeper to reach that milestone.

After being called up for the 2022 FIFA World Cup in Qatar, Neuer became the first German goalkeeper to feature in four consecutive World Cups. On 1 December, he made his 19th World Cup appearance, breaking the previous record for goalkeepers.

2004-2011

FC SCHALKE 04

Neuer played for Schalke 04 II till 2008. Neuer progressed through every age group at his hometown club, Schalke 04, and signed professional terms in 2005. He made his Bundesliga debut with Schalke on matchday 2 of the 2006–07 season.

In November 2009, he was the only German goalkeeper in the list of five nominated goalkeepers for the UEFA Team of the Year.

For the 2010–11 season, Neuer was appointed captain and led the team to its first Champions League semi-final appearance to play against Manchester United. He also won the DFB-Pokal in his final season with the club.

On 20 April 2011, he announced that he would not be extending his contract with Schalke, which was set to expire at the end of the 2011–12 season.

FC BAYERN MUNICH

Neuer is regarded as one of the Greatest Football Players to ever play for Bayern Munich. He is the current captain of the first team.

On 1 June 2011, Neuer made his move to Bayern Munich and signed a five-year contract that lasted until June 2016.

On 25 April 2012, Neuer saved penalty kicks from both Cristiano Ronaldo and Kaká and helped Bayern through the 2011–12 Champions League semi-finals against Real Madrid. Bayern went on to progress to the 2012 UEFA Champions League Final against Chelsea, but lost the match.

Neuer started 2012-13 the season by winning the 2012 DFL-Supercup. In the 2012–13 UEFA Champions League knockout phase, Neuer posted four clean sheets in a row. After losing the final match for the previous season, Bayern Munich won the 2012-13 UEFA Champions League, after defeating Borussia Dortmund in the final. Bayern Munich also won the 2013 UEFA Super Cup, defeating Chelsea in the finals, where Neuer saved the last and decisive penalty. Neuer also won the 2013 FIFA Club World Cup with Bayern. Neuer was announced as the 2013 World Goalkeeper of The Year on 7 January 2014. On 2 May 2014, Neuer extended his contract until the summer of 2019.

In 2014, Neuer won the German Footballer of the Year prize, was voted into the UEFA Team of the Year, and finished third in the FIFA Ballon d'Or.

On 19 July 2017, it was announced that Neuer would become the new captain for both Bayern and the Germany national team. On 13 September 2017, Neuer made his 100th European appearance in a Champions League match. On 21 May 2020, Neuer signed a new contract with Bayern, keeping him at the club until 2023.

On 14 August 2020, Neuer played a crucial part in the 2019-20 UEFA Champions League quarter finals, where Barcelona suffered one of the worst defeats in their history, as Bayern Munich defeated them 8-2. On 23 August, Neuer won the Champions League for the second time, his first as Bayern captain, keeping a clean sheet as his team won 1–0 over PSG. Bayern also won the 2020 UEFA Super Cup and 2020 FIFA Club World Cup with Neuer as captain. In 2020, Bayern won 6 major trophies in one year, being the only club to do so along with FC Barcelona in 2009, all with Manuel Neuer as the club captain.

Neuer's 300th Bundesliga victory came in his 447th Bundesliga game on 23 October 2021. After the season, Neuer extended his contract with Bayern Munich until 2024.
